## Project Overview
This project explores the critical intersection of financial instability and technological decline. By analyzing major historical failures—including the 2008 US Housing Market collapse and the downfalls of tech giants like Yahoo and Nokia—this study draws vital lessons on risk management, economic stability, and project governance.

This work serves as a final project for the **Engineering Economics & Project Management** course, applying theoretical concepts to real-world scenarios.

## Key Chapters
* **Chapter 1 - The Big Short:** An investigation into the greed and systemic risks that led to the US housing market collapse, including the roles of Mortgage-Backed Securities (MBS) and the fall of Lehman Brothers.
* **Chapter 2 - Tech Giants' Mistakes:** An analysis of the strategic missteps and market failures that led to the decline of industry leaders like Yahoo and Nokia.
